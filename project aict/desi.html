Chicken Makhni Handi

Ingredients:chicken, 1 tbsp Oil or ghee,1 large Onion, sliced,2 medium Tomatoes, pureed, 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic paste,1 tbsp Green Chilli paste, 1 tsp Coriander powder, 1/2 tsp Cumin powder,1/4 tsp Turmeric powdeer, 1/2 tsp Garam Masala powder, 1/4 tsp Kashmiri Chilli powder (for color),1 cup Fresh butter,1/2 cup Water or Milk,1 tbsp Butter (for garnishing)

Instructions:Marinate the Chicken: Marinate the chicken pieces as mentioned above. Make the Gravy: Heat oil or ghee in a heavy-bottomed pot. Add sliced onions and saute until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilli paste and saute for a minute. Add Spices: Add coriander powder, cumin powder, turmeric powder, garam masala powder, and Kashmiri chilli powder. Saute for a minute until fragrant. Add Tomatoes: Add tomato puree and cook until the oil separates from the masala. Cook the Chicken: Add the marinated chicken pieces to the gravy and cook until the chicken is tender. Add Cream: Add fresh cream and water or milk. Simmer for 5-7 minutes until the gravy thickens. Garnish: Garnish with butter and fresh coriander.

Prawn Biryani

Ingredients: For Marinating the Prawns: 500g prawns (peeled and deveined) 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste 1 teaspoon turmeric powder 1 teaspoon red chili powder 1 teaspoon garam masala 2 tablespoons yogurt Salt to taste Juice of half a lemon Fresh coriander leaves (optional) For the Rice: 2 cups basmati rice 4 cups water 2 bay leaves 4-5 green cardamom pods 4-5 cloves 1-2 cinnamon sticks 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds Salt to taste 1 tablespoon ghee (clarified butter) For the Biryani Gravy: 2 large onions (thinly sliced) 2 tomatoes (chopped) 2-3 green chilies (slit) 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder 1 teaspoon red chili powder 1 teaspoon garam masala 1 teaspoon cumin powder 1/2 teaspoon coriander powder Fresh coriander leaves (chopped) Fresh mint leaves (chopped) 1/4 cup coconut milk (optional, for richness) 1 tablespoon ghee or oil Salt to taste For Assembling: 1/4 cup fried onions (for garnish) 1 tablespoon ghee or butter (optional, for extra richness) A few saffron strands soaked in warm milk (optional for color and flavor) Optional for Extra Flavor: Rose water or kewra water (a few drops) A squeeze of fresh lime or lemon juice

Instructions: 1. Marinate the Prawns: In a bowl, mix the prawns with ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, yogurt, salt, and lemon juice. Let the prawns marinate for at least 30 minutes. You can also marinate them for a few hours in the fridge for deeper flavor. 2. Cook the Rice: Wash the basmati rice thoroughly until the water runs clear, then soak it in water for 20-30 minutes. In a large pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil. Add the bay leaves, cardamom, cloves, cinnamon sticks, cumin seeds, and salt. Add the soaked rice and cook it until it’s 70% done (the rice should still be firm). Drain the rice and set it aside.

Paneer Sabzi

Ingredients: 200g paneer (cubed) 2 tablespoons oil or ghee 1 large onion (finely chopped) 2 tomatoes (pureed) 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste 2 green chilies (slit, optional) 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder 1 teaspoon red chili powder 1 teaspoon garam masala 1 teaspoon coriander powder 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder Salt to taste 1/2 cup water or as required for desired consistency 1/4 cup fresh cream (optional, for a richer gravy) Fresh coriander leaves (chopped, for garnish)

Instructions: Heat oil/ghee: In a deep pan or kadhai, heat 2 tablespoons of oil or ghee on medium heat. Temper spices: Add cumin seeds and bay leaf (if using). Let them splutter. Sauté onions: Add finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown. This can take about 6-8 minutes on medium heat. Stir occasionally to avoid burning. Add ginger-garlic paste: Once the onions are golden, add 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1-2 minutes until the raw smell disappears. Cook tomatoes: Add the pureed tomatoes to the pan and cook until the oil starts to separate from the masala (about 5-7 minutes). Stir occasionally to ensure it doesn’t stick to the pan.

Champey

Ingredients: 500g mutton ribs (or chops) 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste 1 teaspoon red chili powder 1 teaspoon turmeric powder 1 teaspoon garam masala powder 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder Salt to taste 1/2 cup yogurt

Instructions:Pressure Cooker Method: Add 1-2 cups of water (depending on your desired gravy consistency) and cook the mutton under pressure for about 15-20 minutes (or 3-4 whistles). Slow-Cook Method: If you're using a regular pot, add water and cook the mutton on low heat for 45-60 minutes, stirring occasionally. Keep adding water as needed to maintain the gravy consistency. The meat should become tender and fully cooked by this time. Add optional richness: If you want a richer gravy, grind poppy seeds and melon seeds together to make a smooth paste. Add this paste to the curry in the last 10-15 minutes of cooking for extra creaminess.

Kabuli Pulao

Ingredients: 500g mutton (or chicken) (cut into pieces) 1 large onion (sliced thinly) 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste 1 teaspoon ground cumin 1 teaspoon ground coriander 1 teaspoon ground turmeric 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon 2-3 green cardamom pods 4-5 cloves 1 bay leaf 1/2 teaspoon black pepper Salt to taste 2 tablespoons oil (vegetable or ghee) 2-3 cups water (or chicken stock for extra flavor)

Instructions: Heat oil/ghee: In a large pot, heat 2 tablespoons of oil or ghee over medium heat. Sauté onions: Add the sliced onions and sauté until they become golden brown (about 7-8 minutes). Add ginger-garlic paste: Add 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste to the pot and sauté for 1-2 minutes until fragrant. Add spices: Add cumin, coriander, turmeric, cinnamon, cardamom pods, cloves, bay leaf, black pepper, and salt. Stir well to coat the onions and meat with the spices. Add meat: Add the mutton (or chicken) pieces and sear them until they are browned on all sides. Add water: Add 2-3 cups of water (or chicken stock) to the pot and bring it to a boil. Then lower the heat, cover the pot, and simmer for about 1 hour for mutton (or 30 minutes for chicken), until the meat is tender and cooked through. If using chicken, the cooking time will be shorter. Set the meat aside: Once the meat is cooked, remove the pieces from the broth and set them aside. Strain the broth, if needed, to remove whole spices. 2. Prepare the Rice: Rinse the rice: Wash the basmati rice thoroughly under cold water until the water runs clear. This will remove excess starch. Boil the rice: In a large pot, bring 2 1/2 cups of water to a boil. Add the rice, cinnamon stick, cardamom pods, and cloves. Cook the rice until it is 70-80% cooked (al dente), around 8-10 minutes. Drain the rice and set aside. Fluff the rice: In the same pot, heat 1 tablespoon of ghee or butter. Add the partially cooked rice and gently fluff it up with a fork.
